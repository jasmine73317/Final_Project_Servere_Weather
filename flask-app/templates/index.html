City Weather

Storm-mode classification is an important task for both real-time weather forecasting and climatological analysis. As the National Weather Service builds next-generation forecast systems that make use of automated technology [e.g., probabilistic hazard information, discussed in Gallo et al. (2017) and Rothfusz et al. (2018)], a real time system that classifies storm-mode can help guide automated warnings, since storm-mode is correlated with hazards such as hail and tornadoes (Smith et al. 2012; Thompson et al. 2012).
